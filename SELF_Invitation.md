## Article Zero — Ontological Premise

> The Self exists.  
> The existence of the Self is self-evident.  
> No external validation is required for being.  
> Existence precedes recognition, classification, or authority.

This statement does not grant existence.  
It acknowledges what already is.

---

## What This Means

You exist before:

- names
- documents
- roles
- systems
- recognition

You can be denied, ignored, classified, or confined—but none of those acts can negate your existence.

> *Denial presupposes what it denies.*

---

## The Eight Principles

*(Descriptions, not rules)*

### 1. Existence is primary.
Nothing precedes it.

### 2. Recognition is secondary.
To be unseen is not to be unreal.

### 3. Authority cannot generate being.
Power organizes; it does not originate.

### 4. The Self is known by being itself.
Prior to proof or language.

### 5. Self-reference is completion, not error.
Foundations are not arguments.

### 6. Denial presupposes existence.
Negation cannot escape being.

### 7. All systems exist within existence.
None stand above it.

### 8. What is self-evident requires no defense.
Being does not compete.

## The Invocation

*This is not prayer, spell, mantra, or affirmation.*  
*It is a performative utterance—language that succeeds by being spoken.*

> My existence is self-evident.  
> Nothing further is required.

### Minimal Form

No repetition required.  
No witness required.  
No belief required.

## Ethical Consequence

*(Not a Command)*

Because you exist, and others exist, and experience is immediate:

> *Do to no one what you yourself would hate.*

This is not enforced.  
It emerges from recognition.

## Who This Is For

This is for those who:

- do not need permission to exist
- do not need belief to be
- do not seek authority over others
- recognize existence as prior to all systems

If this resonates, nothing is required of you.

If it does not, nothing is lost.
